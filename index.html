Profile

I am a PhD candidate at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER), Pune. My research focuses on the analytical and computational modeling of stochastic processes and non-equilibrium systems.

With an expertise in high-performance computing and complex fluid dynamics, I specialize in simulations of particle and fluid transport for microfluidic and nanofluidic applications.

Research Experience

Soft Matter

Overdamped Dynamics in Nanochannels

Investigated ratcheting transport phenomena in nanochannels with undulating walls. Studied the efficiency of the ratcheting process and analyzed the effects of physical parameters like temperature on transport behavior.

Fluid Dynamics

Surface-Fluctuation Driven Flow

Developed perturbation analysis for low Reynolds number flow in nanotubes. Successfully distinguished surface-driven flow from bulk flow under pressure head in cylindrical nanochannels.

Systems Biology

Gene Regulatory Networks in EMT

Modeled gene regulatory circuits involved in Epithelial to Mesenchymal Transition (EMT). Analyzed conditions for pattern formation and multistability in complex biological systems.

Stochastic Modeling

Transport in Soft Microchannels

Designed a "hydrodynamic ratchet" model where the fluid flow itself acts as the transport mechanism. Currently extending this to multi-particle systems and polymer-like chains.

Theory

Path Integrals & RG Theory

Conducted research on foundational concepts in quantum field theory, including path integral formulation, group theory, and renormalization group (RG) theory.

Continuous Learning

Stochastic Calculus & Processes

In-depth study of Itô and Stratonovich interpretations, Langevin dynamics, and noise-induced behavior in complex systems.
